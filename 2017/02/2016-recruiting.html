My 2016 recruiting experience

First interview: 8/16 10:30 am PST
Last offer signed: 11/23 9:42 am PST
Companies: 16
Homework challenges: 6
Phone interviews: 18
Onsites: 7

Offers: 9

Before last summer, I knew almost nothing of Silicon Valley and its tech culture. Needless to say, I was very surprised by both how early and how involved the recruiting season was.

Really started last August after attending the Greylock Techfair. The process kicked off when I attended Internapalooza and the Greylock Techfair. Internapalooza is a very large career fair in the Bay Area open to everyone and the Greylock Techfair is a slightly smaller career fair that requires an application. Internapalooza ended up having way too many eager interns to be useful (although I was able to, however, to get a lifetime supply of T-shirts).

After the Greylock Techfair was a lot more useful and helped kicked off my interview season. Kicked off not because of me, kind of thrown into it. Everyone around me was hustling so I wanted to fit in. My first interview I was destroyed. Hackerrank/Leetcode/Cracking the Coding interview are a persons best friend. I went to seattle and san francisco. Homeworks are stressful. Northeastern co-op put me in touch with a lot of cool startups.
